This page is inspired by my love for the classic Sierra RPG/adventure game Quest for Glory IV: Shadows of Darkness, first released in 1994. Sierra was also known for other popular adventure game series such as King's Quest, Space Quest and Police Quest. Quest for Glory IV takes place in the fictional land of Mordavia, based on Russia or Eastern Europe judging from the characters' names, accents, and the elements of folk mythology incorporated into the story. The game is notable not only for its gameplay, which follows the basic model of all previous games in the series, but for its excellent music and its visual aesthetic. Some consider the title one of the best adventure games of all time. The man you might see to your left depending on your screen width is Dr. Cranium, a helpful scientist character from the game, while the background image is the inside of his lab.

My main purpose here is to follow up on an idea which came to me last year in the context of a user interface course in graduate school. Namely this is an attempt to imitate much of the interface of the game, including panel images and menu graphics, but to apply it to a website rather than a game. Others have already implemented some of the cursors from the game as extensions to an operating system. Much more information and many more downloads relevant to the series can be found at Quest for More Glory.

Downloads

All five of the original games in the series can be had cheaply from GOG.com. This set of the games includes a remake of the first title with far better graphics, but unfortunately those of the second game are still rather primitive. You can however download a 2011 remake of Quest for Glory II: Trial by Fire. This is freely available, as is a well-received 2013 game based on the series, entitled Heroine's Quest. There is also a laughably awful fan-made game called Quest for Glory 4 1/2, released in 2012, and a 2014 game similar to but not explicitly based on the series called Quest for Infamy. Many more downloads can be found at a fan page called Quest for More Glory.

A "role-playing adventure game" entitled Hero U is currently under development by Sierra designers Corey and Lori Cole, who were responsible for the Quest for Glory series and contributed to various other Sierra games such as King's Quest V. Another apparently unfinished effort at a QfG-inspired game, this one entitled Hero 6, can be found here.

Icons

Here are some icons from the game, plus a bullet image I may be able to implement in the dropdown menu. These are used to interact with the environment in different ways; for example, the mouth icon is used to speak with someone, while the feet icon in the menu above is used to walk somewhere. An attempt has already been made by others to implement some of these as extensions to an operating system.

Music

The music for this game has been described as "gothic, folk and rock." According to the composer, Aubrey Hodges, it is not yet available in sheet music form. The original soundtrack is however freely available from Sierra Music Central or on YouTube, while an updated version including live instruments and 42 new arrangements can be purchased from the composer's Bandcamp page.
